Animals

Animals are an essential part of this ecosystem as they help in balancing the environment. There are over 7 million animal species found in the world. All animals are mainly divided into 5 types: Mammals, Birds, Reptiles, Amphibians, and Insects.

Types of Animals

These are the 5 main types of animals:

  1. Mammals
  2. Reptiles
  3. Birds
  4. Amphibians
  5. Insects
  6. Aquatic Animals

Mammals   ↑Top

Mammals are warm-blooded vertebrates that have hair or fur on their bodies and feed their young ones with milk. Examples include humans, lions, and elephants


Reptiles   ↑Top

Reptiles are cold-blooded vertebrates that have dry, scaly skin and usually lay eggs on land. They breathe through lungs and rely on external heat sources to regulate their body temperature. Examples include snakes, lizards, crocodiles, and turtles.


Bird  ↑Top

Birds are warm-blooded vertebrates that have feathers, wings, and beaks. They lay eggs and most of them can fly, although some are flightless. Birds have hollow bones that make their bodies lightweight. Examples include sparrows, eagles, and penguins.


Amphibians   ↑Top

Amphibians are cold-blooded vertebrates that can live both in water and on land. They usually have moist skin and breathe through their skin, lungs, or gills at different stages of life. Amphibians lay eggs in water. Examples include frogs, toads, and salamanders.

Aquatic Animals  ↑Top

Aquatic animals are organisms that live in water for most or all of their lives. They can be found in both freshwater (like rivers and lakes) and saltwater (like seas and oceans). Aquatic animals breathe through gills or lungs, depending on the species. Examples include fish, dolphins, whales, and jellyfish.
